Aaron Ngai

Graphic & UI/UX Design

95901

What intrigued you to Design & why did you choose the field you are in now?

I was never really interested in Design till my senior year of High School where we had a simple Graphic Design class. I took it for units and grew to love it because of the endless creativity and freedom I felt from just doing homework. As I went through college UX/UI struck me as something I was more into as I went through classes and done my own research.

What have been your biggest struggles of your career?

The biggest struggle for me is having to chose between family and my career. Having a big ties to my family for who I am puts a lot of strain and stress on the work I do. When I was in a group project for one of my classes I had a situation where I had to be with my family and needed my group to continue working without me for the time being. Sadly that didn't work out that way and I had moments where I needed to decide if I could do all this work or be with my family. I did both which was a huge strain on my health.

What have your experiences been like as a person of color in the design industry? Does those experiences shape how you solve design-related problems?

I guess that word would be interesting. I've been through a few moments where clients would question my ability in design. As an Asian, a lot of people (in my area) see me as someone doing something greater than design or running with tradition and it gets pretty annoying and tiresome but that's just a few people in one town verse a whole country. So I don't think too much about it.
